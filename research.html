Research
Current Research

I am currently studying the mechanism of lipid nanoparticle (LNP) uptake and drug delivery in cells in Professor Markita Landry's laboratory at UC Berkeley. We are working to particularly elucidate the mechanism of endosomal escape of mRNA from LNPs and study protein corona interactions with mRNA-LNP complexes via in vitro mammalian cell culture techniques, isolation and extraction methods, Western blot analyses, and assays. I am currently writing an Honors Thesis on this work.

Past Research

I first started my scientific research journey in September 2023 in Professor Michael Marletta's lab, where I worked on expressing, purifying, and characterizing a metalloenzyme called a soluble guanylate cyclase (sGC) that senses both nitric oxide (NO) and diatomic oxygen.

From June-August 2025, I participated in the Summer Research Opportunities Program (SROP) at Northwestern University. I worked in Professor Samuel Stupp's lab, where I evaluated the cytotoxicity and bioactivity of novel bottlebrush peptide amphiphile biomaterials that are supposed to aid with cartilage regeneration. This research culminated in a research report and a poster that I presented at the 2025 Northwestern SROP Research Symposium.
